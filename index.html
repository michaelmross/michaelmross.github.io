Experimental Mathematics

  • Congruence Entropy and Prime-Rich Proximate-Prime Quadratics

    A dataset of 2,221 "proximate-prime polynomials" — quadratics through consecutive prime quadruples whose gaps fall in arithmetic progression — shows that their prime-richness is almost entirely explained by local congruence structure — the single nonresidue count S(D) accounts for ~60% of the variance, the nine Legendre symbols used individually ~76%, and a full local-obstruction model ~96%. The mechanism is the quantitative, family-wide form of the classical Rabinowitsch/Heegner observation: a discriminant that is a nonresidue mod many small primes has no roots there, hence a large Bateman–Horn constant. Control experiments show the construction genuinely selects for such low-obstruction discriminants rather than merely picking favorably sized quadratics.

  • Primes in Square Intervals: The Remaining Analytic Obstacle

    The spine of the Jn analytic program. A five-stage Bombieri–Friedlander–Iwaniec dispersion chain reduces prime existence in Jn to a single averaged-Kloosterman hypothesis in the Deshouillers–Iwaniec style. The perfect-square centering at (2n)2 places a square factor in the second Kloosterman argument; the precise analytic consequence of that structure is sharpened in the continuation paper.
